Advanced and Troubleshooting

Power​

Sometimes the system is shut down in the incorrect order or an individual device is shut off independently.

Tip

Please do not shut anything off except for the items outlined in Shutting Down

No Audio​

If there is no audio make sure each of the following devices are on and proceed to the Checking Audio Inputs section below

  • Sound Board
    • Switch on rear behind screen
  • EQ Processor inside rack
    • Power Switch on right side
      • Just beneath Main Power Switch
  • Speaker Amplifier
    • Switch on right side
      • Located at the bottom of the rack

Sometimes these items get switched off Some pieces of equipment can be individually switched off. These Should Not but sometimes they do get switched off. If you are experiencing issues make sure that all items are on

Video Sources​

The Extron Scan Converter to the right of the monitor will display numbers on the screen if the computer is outputting signal to the monitor and projector. If there is not a video signal it will read "No Signal". The Scan Converter converts the signal from the computer to analog video for the projector, it also provides the video signal to the monitor


Audio Sources​

Checking Audio Inputs​

The Channel Meters are located above each channels mute button. The Meters show visually the input level of the channel even if it is muted. The green level indicators should sit comfortably at the 12:00 position and should not take up the entire circle.

If the meter shows too low or too high make an adjustment to the Gain setting according to the section below.


Checking Audio Outputs​

Make sure the Master fader is set to U. If this slider is all the way down there's gonna be no sound.


The output meter shows the audio levels on the output channels of the board


Adjusting Audio Gain/Sensitivity​

Gain is the input sensitivity of an audio channel. Gain is adjusted with the knobs along the top of the console.

  • OL - Overload
    • Shows if a channels input is being overloaded and needs to be adjusted to a lower gain
  • SIG - Signal
    • Shows that a channel has a signal

  • LINE
    • Changes the input from a mic level input to a line level input. These should not be changed.
  • 48V
    • Enables 48 volt phantom power for Condenser microphones that require a 'bias voltage'. We do not use these mics regularly in this space and 48V should not be enabled.
Take care

Enabling 48V on sources not designed to accept phantom power like CD players, Keyboards, and other electronic sources can cause damage. Normal dynamic microphones will not be effected by this setting.
